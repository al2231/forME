Mirena

The Mirena intrauterine device (IUD) contains levonorgestrel -Â  a female hormone that can cause changes in your cervix and uterus.

T-shaped plastic intrauterine device.

Can prevent pregnancy for up to 8 years.

Can be used for up to 5 years to treat heavy menstrual bleeding.

May be used with or without children.

Warnings

Should not be used as emergency birth control.

DO NOT use during pregnancy. Tell your doctor right away if you do become pregnant.

Do not use Mirena if you have allergies to levonorgestrel, silicone, silica, silver, barium, iron oxide, or polyethylene.
